Arima - > Auto regression + moving avg
Autoregression is the time series forcasting method in which 
the current value of variable is predicted using its own past value.

ex- today's temp can be estimated using temp from previous days . 
yt=c+Φyt-1+εt
yt= > current value
yt-1=previous value
c=constant
Φ= > autoregressive coefficient
εt -> random error

Φ>0  - >+ve relationship , if he previous value increases , the current value trend 
to increase
Φ<0 -> ve relationship 

if the previous value increases , the current value trends to decrease
Φ=0 : no relationship between current and previous value



|Φ|<1 - > the series is generally stable 
Auto regressive coefficient indicates the strength and direction of the influence of past
value on the present value. 


Φ=summation of (yt-1-ybar)(yt-ybar)
--------------------------------------
(yt-1-ybar)^2
yt= current value
yt-1 = previous value
ybar= mean of the series





moving average (ma) - > statistical method used to smooth out short term fluctuation in a time series and

it is calculated by taking avg of fixed no of consecutive obs and moving this window through the data

stationarity
-> stationary time series is one whose statistical properties do not change over time
A data series is stationary if
its mean is constant over time
its variance is constant over time
its pattern of changes (autocorelation does not depend on time)

example - > daily temp after removing the seasonal effect 
completely random data around a constant mean
non stationary 
stock price ( they keep increase or decrease over time)
sales data with strong trend or seasonality

why important


most forcasting model like arima work properly only on stationary data

stationary means the data behavs the same over the time , no trend , no changing spread. 
it means 

no long term trend
no changing variation
no repeating seasonal pattern

type of stationarity
strict stationarity -> entire statistical structure remains the same over time
very strong condition (rare in real data)
weak stationarity(most used)
1. mean is const
2. variance is cosnstant
3. covarinnce depends only on lag , not time .
